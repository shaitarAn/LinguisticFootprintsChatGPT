Zika virus is primarily transmitted through the bite of infected Aedes mosquitoes. It is endemic in many tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, where this patient resided. The symptoms of Zika virus infection typically include fever, rash, joint pain, and conjunctivitis. In some cases, patients may also experience headache, muscle pain, and vomiting.

Although the symptoms of Zika virus infection are usually mild and self-limiting, there have been reports of severe complications, particularly in pregnant women. Zika virus infection during pregnancy has been associated with an increased risk of birth defects, including microcephaly. Therefore, it is important to closely monitor pregnant women who have been infected with Zika virus.
