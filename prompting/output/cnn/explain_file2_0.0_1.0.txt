This news article is about the tragic bombings that occurred at the Boston Marathon. The bombings resulted in the loss of lives and many injuries. The victims included a 29-year-old woman, a graduate student from China, and an 8-year-old boy. Thirteen others had limbs amputated as a result of the explosions.

Authorities are still trying to determine who is responsible for the attack. Unlike previous terrorist attacks, no one has claimed responsibility and no suspects have been identified yet. The bombs used in the attack were made from pressure cookers filled with nails and pellets, causing extensive damage.

The investigation into the bombings is ongoing, but regardless of what is discovered, it cannot take away the pain experienced by those affected by this tragedy. Many people are facing physical recovery as well as psychological trauma from witnessing or being involved in the blasts.

Despite this horrific event, there were also acts of heroism displayed by individuals who rushed to help those injured in the aftermath of the explosions. Spectators and runners alike stepped up to provide aid to those in need.

Authorities have provided some details about how these bombs were constructed but have not yet determined who was behind them. Initial tests suggest that they were crude devices made with simple materials rather than high-grade explosives.

The article concludes by mentioning some of those who lost their lives in this tragedy - an 8-year-old boy named Martin Richard, a 29-year-old woman named Krystle Campbell, and a Boston University graduate student from China whose identity has not been released.

Many survivors owe their lives to emergency personnel and volunteers on-site as well as medical professionals at nearby hospitals who provided immediate care for their injuries caused by nails and metal beads used in these bombs.
