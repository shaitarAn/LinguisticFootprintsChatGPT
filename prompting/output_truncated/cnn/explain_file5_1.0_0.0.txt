This news article is about Louisiana Governor Bobby Jindal considering a run for the presidency in 2016. He mentions that he is thinking and praying about whether to run, and he will make his final decision after the November midterm elections. Jindal states that his decision to run will be based on whether he believes he can make a difference and if he has unique ideas or experiences to offer.

During a breakfast event, Jindal also unveils an energy proposal from his nonprofit group, America Next. The plan emphasizes the importance of developing domestic oil and gas resources, including through hydraulic fracturing. Jindal criticizes the Obama administration's opposition to the Keystone XL pipeline, calling them "science deniers" when it comes to American energy potential.

Jindal also addresses other topics during the breakfast event. He expresses support for the administration's efforts to combat the Ebola outbreak in Africa and believes the US has a humanitarian and vested interest in helping affected countries. Regarding ISIS, Jindal believes Congress should support the White House's proposals to train and arm Syrian rebels. However, he criticizes the administration for allowing Islamic extremists to grow unchecked due to their repeated delays.

Jindal touches on subjects that might be popular with Republican primary voters, such as the need for spending controls and concerns about the perceived "war on religious liberty" in the US. He also states that President Obama is the worst since Jimmy Carter, claiming that Obama lacks belief in American exceptionalism and is the most ideologically radical and incompetent president in his lifetime.
